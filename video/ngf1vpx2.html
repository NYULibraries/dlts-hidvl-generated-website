Synopsis: Upon the death of Pope Jean Paul II, the city of Montréal--no longer a bastion of the Catholic faith but a predominately atheistic contemporary city--became obsessed with the election of the next pontiff. Despite the schism with the traditions of the church, citizens nightly looked towards the top of Mont Royal, upon which a massive 100 ft illuminated crucifix looms over the city, its lights switched to a Byzantine purple in recognition of the recently deceased Pope. The city waited, contemplating its relationship to Catholicism, until the new pontiff was selected and the giant cross once again lit up in its normal nightly state of bright white light. This public ritual, and the spectacle which unfolded in Vatican City, was the inspiration for this short lip synch cabaret performance that uses audio clips from the film All I Desire (directed by Douglas Sirk, with a monologue spoken by Barbara Stanwyck) and a stirring rendition by Ethel Merman of "There's No Business Like Show Business." Large scale video projections created by Aaron Pollard provide a kaleidoscopic background of images that underline the 2boys.tv caustic examination of the subordinate role of women in the church, the theatrical aesthetics of religious rites, and the obsession with the men who rule the institution.2boys.tv is a transdisciplinary duo from Montreal composed of Stephen Lawson and Aaron Pollard (also notoriously known in some circles as alter egos Gigi L'Amour and Pipi Douleur). Emerging in 2001 from Montreal's wildly eclectic cabaret scene, 2boys.tv has since toured their wide repertoire of epic multimedia cabaret works and taught multidisciplinary creations internationally in museums, galleries, theatres, artist-run centres, universities, and festivals. Political expression rolled into subversive and amusing forms is key to their practice as they search for ways to infuse their compositions with passion and the shameless desire for the emancipation of the powerless and displaced within and among us.
